# Section Intro

## Data for Generative AI overview

Data for generative AI, a critical part of building high-quality production-ready gen AI systems. This part of the course will focus on how to prepare, manipulate, store, and optimize data that powers your models, vector stores, and knowledge bases.

## Structured data and transformation tools

We'll begin with the fundamentals of working with structured data and walk through how to transform and prepare it using tools like Amazon Bedrock's data manipulation features, SageMaker Data Wrangler, and AWS Glue.

## Glue Studio, data quality, and monitoring

You'll get hands-on experience with Glue Studio, Glue Data Quality, and using Amazon CloudWatch metrics to monitor your data pipelines.

## Text and audio processing

Then we'll explore how AWS services like Transcribe and Comprehend help process text and audio data, including an in-depth hands-on look at entity extraction, sentiment analysis, and other preprocessing tasks that improve downstream model performance.

## Vector databases and OpenSearch

From there, we'll dive into the core of GenAI data architecture, vector databases. You'll learn how Amazon OpenSearch handles indexing, performance tuning, stability, serverless deployments, and multimodal FM integrations.

## Relational and NoSQL options

We'll also explore relational and NoSQL options, including Amazon RDS, Aurora with PGVector, and DynamoDB, and how they fit into GenAI workloads.

## Search architecture and metadata for RAG

Next, we'll cover how to keep your vector stores accurate and fresh, how to design advanced search architectures, and how to craft metadata frameworks that dramatically improve retrieval quality for rag systems.

## S3 storage and lifecycle

Finally, we'll look at how to store and manage data efficiently with Amazon S3, including storage classes, lifecycle rules, and cross-region replication, ensuring your data is secure, cost-optimized, and highly available.

## Section outcomes

By the end of this section, you'll have a complete understanding of how to manage the full data pipeline for generative AI, from ingestion and transformation to indexing, retrieval, and long-term storage. Let's get started.
